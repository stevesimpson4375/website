Some personal information

My Hobbies are:

  1. Cycling
    I didn't start cycling until a few years ago. I had a co-worker that was a competitive cyclist and used to ride his carbon fiber bike to work. This was in 2009 and I had just given up smoking and thought cycling would help me get healthier.
  2. Gaming
    I have been gaming since I was a small child. I do not quite recall what year it was, but I had an SNES when they were very new. Prior to that I had a Tandy from RadioShack. I spent many hours playing games like Arkanoid. Sadly I missed out on PC gaming in the 90s. I only played console games and did not get to enjoy more complex PC games. Today I am about 95% PC gaming only and I am contemplating buying a Radeon 290X in preparation for GTA V.
  3. Cars
    I inherited the hobby of driving and working on cars from my father. I used to hear about people who buy a project car and let it sit for years and think "Why on earth would you do that?". Today, I know why. I bought a 1974 Corvette in 2011 and have barely touched it. Working on the other cars I need to use to get places and things like work and school have caused a significant delay in fixing up that car. My current project is finishing repairs to a 1995 Eagle Talon that I have had since high school. It has had a rough life including a brief moment upside down. I always liked that car and it gets fantastic fuel mileage. I am planning to make it my next driver.

Honorable mentions:

I have been trying to work on a number of other hobbies, however I do not include them when asked because I did not really succeed. For instance, I have played guitar of and on for years now. I can play a number of things, however I never learned to read music and forgot more than I know.

I have also purchased a motorcycle and attempted to ride it. Actually learning to ride is still on my to-do list. My initial attempt was ended due to an issue with the clutch. I bought a used motorcycle from a dealer that was slightly cheaper than the same motorcycle everywhere else. I would have tried to argue that the dealer obviously knew of the issue since riding motorcycles that are for sale is necessary, if only to move them around. However I bought it in January and didn't try to ride it until April. I do not think it will be expensive or difficult to fix, however I had an incident where I injured my knee and I came to the realization that hitting the asphalt would REALLY hurt.
